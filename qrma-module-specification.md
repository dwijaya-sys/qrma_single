# QRMA Screening Dashboard v2 — Module Specification & Evidence Reference

> **Document type:** Developer + AI handover specification  
> **Scope:** 8 functional modules — parameter tables, scoring logic, scientific rationale, confirmatory tests, food-first actions  
> **Last updated:** May 2026  
> **Critical framing:** This dashboard is a **screening tool and hypothesis generator only**. It makes no diagnostic claims. All outputs must be described using hedged language: "pattern suggests," "screening flag," "confirm with." No disease names, no exact probability estimates.

## How to Read This Document

Two tables are provided for each section of the dashboard:

- **Table A — Parameter Reference:** One row per input parameter. Covers scoring direction, rationale, evidence, confirmatory tests, and food actions.
- **Table B — Module Reference:** One row per module. Covers goal, score logic, alert thresholds, UI recommendations, and developer notes.

Confidence labels used throughout:

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Well-supported** | Backed by standard clinical guidelines (e.g., KDIGO, IDF, AHA). The parameter or pattern is used in mainstream clinical screening. |
| **Exploratory** | There is a plausible biological mechanism and some supporting research, but the parameter is not yet a validated standalone clinical biomarker. Use with caution. |
| **Needs lab confirmation** | The QRMA reading is an indirect proxy signal. A screening flag here should prompt, not replace, a standard laboratory test. |

## Scientific Evidence Overview

Before the tables, a brief orientation to the evidence base behind each domain.

### Allostatic Load & Multisystem Stress (Modules 1)
Allostatic load (AL) is defined as the cumulative "wear and tear" on body systems from chronic stress and inefficient adaptation, a concept formalised by McEwen and Stellar in 1993. Systematic reviews confirm that AL indices composed of cardiovascular, metabolic, neuroendocrine, and immune biomarkers predict mortality, cognitive decline, and disability independent of age. The scoring approach used in Module 1 — flagging biomarkers beyond population-based tertile cut-offs across multiple systems — mirrors the most widely validated AL calculation method.

### Oxidative Stress & Antioxidant Reserve (Module 2)
Oxidative stress occurs when reactive oxygen species (ROS) production exceeds the body's antioxidant defences. Glutathione, CoQ10, vitamin C, vitamin E, and selenium are the principal antioxidant molecules whose depletion is associated with increased lipid peroxidation (measured as MDA/TBARS in research settings). Meta-analyses confirm that CoQ10 supplementation significantly reduces MDA and increases total antioxidant capacity (TAC) and superoxide dismutase (SOD) activity. Combined selenium and CoQ10 supplementation over 48 months significantly improved systemic redox status in clinical trials. However, the direct clinical outcome benefit of antioxidant supplementation (i.e., reduction in cardiovascular events or mortality) remains contested, and these markers are not validated standalone biomarkers in mainstream clinical screening. QRMA readings for these parameters are indirect proxies.

### Heavy Metals & Environmental Exposure (Module 3)
Lead, mercury, cadmium, and arsenic are classified by the WHO as priority public health toxicants. There is no known safe blood lead level; even levels below 5 µg/dL are associated with cardiovascular, renal, and developmental effects in dose-dependent fashion. The WHO attributed over 1.5 million deaths globally in 2021 to lead exposure, primarily cardiovascular. Confirmation requires whole blood or urine speciation testing; QRMA signals are not independently validated for heavy metal quantification and must be treated as exposure flags only.

### Metabolic Risk (Module 4)
The harmonised metabolic syndrome definition (IDF/AHA/WHO, 2009) requires three of five criteria: elevated waist circumference, raised triglycerides (≥150 mg/dL), low HDL, raised fasting glucose (≥100 mg/dL), and raised blood pressure. This is the strongest-evidence module. Waist circumference is independently associated with each metabolic syndrome component and cardiovascular risk. HbA1c, fasting lipid panels, and fasting glucose are the gold-standard confirmatory tests.

### Cardio-Renal Strain (Module 5)
KDIGO 2024 guidelines specify that CKD screening must include both eGFR (using the 2021 CKD-EPI creatinine ± cystatin C equation) and urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (ACR). eGFR <60 mL/min/1.73m² and ACR >30 mg/g, confirmed on two separate occasions at least 3 months apart, define CKD. Arterial stiffness (pulse wave velocity) independently predicts cardiovascular morbidity and mortality. Uric acid (>7 mg/dL in men, >6 mg/dL in women) is associated with cardiovascular disease, hypertension, and CKD in epidemiological and Mendelian randomisation studies, though causal independence from other risk factors remains debated.

### Nutritional Deficiency Screening (Module 6)
Vitamin D (25-OH), serum zinc, serum magnesium, serum folate, and serum B12 are standard laboratory markers routinely ordered in clinical practice. Urinary iodine concentration is the WHO-recommended population-level iodine status indicator. B6 deficiency is assessed via plasma pyridoxal-5′-phosphate (PLP). Silicon, as measured by QRMA, has no validated clinical biomarker equivalent. QRMA readings for these nutrients are proxies; laboratory confirmation is required.

### Skin / Collagen & Barrier Function (Module 7)
Transepidermal water loss (TEWL), corneometry (skin hydration), cutometry (elasticity), and sebumeter readings are validated dermatological research tools used in clinical trials. Oral collagen supplementation trials show significant improvements in skin hydration, elasticity, and wrinkle reduction in systematic reviews and RCTs. Skin oxidative stress (free radical signal) is a proxy for ROS activity; direct ROS measurement uses electron paramagnetic resonance (EPR) or fluorescent probes not available in routine clinical settings. QRMA skin readings are indirect proxies.

## Known Limitations & Developer Caveats

1. **QRMA is not independently validated as a clinical diagnostic device.** All module outputs must be framed as hypothesis-generating proxies, not measurements.
2. **Dual-role parameters** (Magnesium and Potassium appear in both Module 5 and Module 6): ensure the input field is shared and the same value propagates to both modules. Do not ask the user to enter them twice.
3. **Sebum in Module 7 is bidirectional**: the alert logic must handle both very low (≤3, dry barrier risk) and very high (≥8, excess oil pattern) as distinct alert types. Most other parameters are unidirectional.
4. **Silicon (Module 6.5)** has the weakest scientific evidence of any parameter in the dashboard. It should carry a UI tooltip stating: "Evidence for silicon as a clinical biomarker is very limited. This score should not be used for clinical decision-making."
5. **Asian-specific waist circumference thresholds** (IDF 2006: men ≥90 cm, women ≥80 cm) apply to Indonesian users. Do not use the European IDF defaults (men ≥94 cm, women ≥80 cm for women, ≥102 for men).
6. **Bio Age Gap formula** in Module 1 is a QRMA-app convention, not a peer-reviewed formula. Label it as an "illustrative estimate."
7. **Module 8 disclaimer cannot be hidden.** This is a product safety requirement, not a UX preference.
